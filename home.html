Healthcare Expenditure From 2010-2017

  • US spends the most on Healthcare.

  • Health spending in the U.S. increased by 4.3% in 2016 to $3.3 trillion or $10,348 per capita. This growth rate is lower than what was observed in 2015 (5.8%) and 2014 (5.1%) and closer to the growth rates over the 5-year period ending in 2013 which averaged 3.8% per year. Spending for 2017 rose faster than in 2016, when it rose 4.3 percent.

  • After USA, Japan spends the most on Healthcare which is about $597 billion. Japan is taking a multi-sectoral and community-based approach to meet the care needs of the ageing population After Japan, Germany France and UK spend the highest for healthcare but not as much as USA.

Healthcare Expenditure as percentage of GDP

  • U.S. spent about 6% of its GDP on health, similar to spending by several comparable countries (the average of comparably wealthy countries was 5% of GDP in 2016).

  • The U.S. was on pace with other countries until the 2000s, when its health spending grew at a significantly faster rate relative to its GDP. In 2017, the U.S. spent 17.15% of its GDP on health, whereas the next highest comparable country (Germany and France) devoted less than 12% of its GDP to health.

Top 5 Countries Share of GDP as percentage in 2017

  • When comparing the Top 5 counties that spend the most on Healthcare, USA is the highest spender with 28% more than the other countires like France, Germany, Japan and Canada who are the next biggest spenders on healthcare

Healthcare per capita vs GDP per capita

  • Wealthy countries like the U.S., tend to spend more per person on health care and related expenses than lower income countries. However, even as a high-income country, the U.S. spends more per person on health than comparable countries. Health spending per person in the U.S. was $10,348 in 2016 which is 30% higher than Germany, the next highest per capita spender.

Healthcare Expense Per Person

  • On average, other wealthy countries spend about half as much per person on health than the U.S. spends Because health spending is closely associated with a country’s wealth, the remaining charts compare the U.S. to similar countries – those that have above median national incomes (as measured by GDP) and also have above median income per person. The average amount spent on health per person in comparable countries ($5551) is just over half that of the U.S. ($10,348).

Healthcare Expense in Public and Private Sectors

  • US healthcare expenditure in Public and Private sectors is triple when compared to other countries.

  • In 2016, the US spent about 8.5% of its GDP on health out of public funds –essentially equivalent to the average of the other comparable countries. However, private spending in the U.S. is much higher than any comparable country; 8.8% of GDP in the U.S., compared to 2.7% on average for other nations.
